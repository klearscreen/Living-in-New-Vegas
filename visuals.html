Requirements:
  • A working installation of the Core module
  • 1 gigabyte of free drive space (Low option), 4.5 gigabytes (Medium option), and 8.5 gigabytes (High option)

The Goal of the Visuals Module:

The goal of this module is to improve the looks of New Vegas greatly, with different options for all ranges of PC specs. Keep in mind that Fallout New Vegas is a game from 2010, and even looked bad for its time. You will never be able to make this game look like a modern AAA game, which is why this guide does not use broken, unoptimized, and frankly ugly tools such as ENBs. All mods here in this module are carefully picked out as to not lower performance much if at all, depending on your specs.

Module Options:

The Visuals module has three options to choose from depending on your system specs; low, medium, and high:

  • Low - No major texture overhauls, just small visual enhancements and new lighting. Very small performance impact if any
  • Medium - Texture overhauls for major objects, such as architecture, landscape, common clutter items, common firearms, and improved lighting/weathers as well. Small performance hit
  • High - Texture overhauls for most common objects in the game, improved lighting/weathers, and new flora/trees/grass. Medium performance hit

Recommended Specs:
  • For the Low option, just about any system that can run the Core module can handle it
  • For the Medium option, any semi-modern PC with a quad-core (or dual-core with hyperthreading) that has decent single-core performance, 6+ GB of RAM, and a GPU with 3GB+ of VRAM can handle it
  • For the High option, pretty much any PC that can run the Medium option can run it, just with a potentially higher performance hit

My System Specs:

  • I personally run the Low option because I like to keep consistency with the style/resolution of textures and have consistently perfect performance/stability. However, my system could run the High option just fine
